# transformers.js · DistilBERT ~65MB

sentiment analysis

what's happening here?

DistilBERT is a small transformer, the same architecture family as today's LLMs distilled down to ~66M parameters. Your sentence is split into tokens, passed through attention layers, and reduced to a two-way score. The percentage is the model's confidence, not ground truth; try a sarcastic sentence and watch it wobble.

# transformers.js · ViT ~90MB

image classification

Drop an image or click to upload
what's happening here?

ViT chops your image into 16×16-pixel patches and treats them like words in a sentence: vision handled as language. The top-4 list is the model's actual probability distribution, not a single verdict.

# WebLLM · WebGPU

chat with a language model — 100% local

Runs a small instruct model on your machine via WebGPU. First launch downloads the weights (~400MB–900MB, then cached). Zero servers, zero API keys.

what's happening here?

The "q4f16" in the model name means the weights are quantized to 4 bits; that's how a billion-parameter model fits in browser memory. The streaming you see is the real generation loop, and the tokens/sec readout is your own hardware's inference speed.
