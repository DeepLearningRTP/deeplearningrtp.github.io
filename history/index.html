About this record

Deep Learning RTP began on February 9, 2017, as a study circle working through fast.ai's Practical Deep Learning for Coders — at the time a months-old course and one of the few practical entry points into the field. The group met across the Research Triangle: Hunt Library at NC State's Centennial Campus in Raleigh, American Underground in Durham, The Frontier in Research Triangle Park, and venues in Chapel Hill and Carrboro.

Its format stayed constant across eight years: bring a laptop, study together, and take turns presenting whatever was new — papers, code, demos, and projects. In-person meetings paused from early 2020 through early 2022 during the COVID-19 pandemic and resumed in March 2022. This page compiles all 400 events from the group's Meetup records.

Ahead of the curve

What the group did, week to week

The headline events sit on top of a much larger base of ordinary meetings, and the records suggest this recurring work is what the group did best. The Raleigh study group alone met 96 times; Durham accounted for 28 more, and a standing crossover with the TriPython community ran for 20 sessions. Nearly every recurring listing opened with the same sentence:

"Bring a laptop and let's study together."

The pattern repeats in the projects: a $250 DonkeyCar built and trained across the fall of 2017, simulated self-driving races in 2022, a do-it-yourself AlphaZero, monthly neural-network coding workshops through 2024. The group's characteristic mode was not commentary but reproduction — taking the field's latest results and getting them running, in public, with whoever showed up.
