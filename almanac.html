A study group, not a lecture series. Between sessions, work through a course or project at your own pace. Wednesdays are where you bring what it raised: the parts that clicked, the parts that didn't, the thing you built to find out. All experience levels and professional backgrounds are welcome.

Transformers.js · DistilBERT ~65MB

Sentiment analysis

What's happening here?

DistilBERT is a small transformer, the same architecture family as today's LLMs distilled down to ~66M parameters. The percentage is the model's confidence, not ground truth; try a sarcastic sentence and watch it wobble.

Transformers.js · ViT ~90MB

Image classification

Drop an image or click to upload
What's happening here?

ViT chops your image into 16×16-pixel patches and treats them like words in a sentence: vision handled as language. The top-4 list is the model's actual probability distribution.

WebLLM · WebGPU

Chat with a language model, 100% local

Runs a small instruct model on your machine via WebGPU. First launch downloads the weights (~400MB–900MB, then cached). Zero servers, zero API keys.

What's happening here?

The "q4f16" in the model name means the weights are quantized to 4 bits; that's how a billion-parameter model fits in browser memory. The tokens/sec readout is your own hardware's inference speed.
