# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary: curious newcomers in the Research Triangle — people who heard about the group and are deciding whether to show up to a first Wednesday session. Secondary: potential speakers, members deciding whether to lead a session. Returning members use the site too (next topic, RSVP, calendar), but the site's job is winning over the first two.

## Product Purpose

The web front door for Deep Learning RTP, a free weekly study group in Research Triangle Park that reads papers and builds neural networks. The site exists to replace Meetup as the group's primary presence and to convert visitors into attendees, subscribers, and session leaders. Success looks like: people showing up on Wednesdays (in person or Zoom), the email list growing, more members volunteering to present, and the site fully standing on its own without Meetup.

## Positioning

A study group, not a lecture series — you participate, you don't spectate. Every page reinforces that sessions are member-led discussion, not talks delivered to an audience.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: deliberately multi-channel — Danny doesn't yet know which channel will prove most effective, so Luma RSVP, the .ics calendar feed, the email group, and Discord are all first-class doors. Don't collapse them into one funnel yet.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "They've been at it for years" — 120+ sessions since 2017, a durable institution, not a pop-up AI meetup.
- Belief ladder (newcomers): (1) this group is real and active — next meetings are visible; (2) it's for people at my level — all levels, discussion not lecture; (3) showing up is easy and low-risk — free, hybrid, no signup wall; then connect via whichever channel suits.
- Proof on hand: the session archive (120 past sessions 2017–2024, linked from the site) and real photos of sessions. Photos not yet in the repo — collect into `.impeccable/assets/proof/`.

## Brand Personality

Rigorous, welcoming, unpretentious. Serious material with zero gatekeeping and no hype. The tone of a group that's been quietly reading papers together for years and is glad you came.

## Anti-references

The hype-y crypto/AI landing page: big claims, countdown urgency, "join the revolution" copy. Nothing on this site should manufacture excitement the group's track record can't back.

## Design Principles

- Proof over pitch — the archive, the live meeting list, and working demos do the persuading; claims never outrun evidence.
- Practice what we preach — the site itself runs neural networks in the visitor's browser; the medium demonstrates the subject.
- Durability reads as trust — lead with the since-2017 track record; nothing urgent, nothing salesy.
- Every door open — Luma, calendar feed, email, Discord are parallel entrances, not a funnel; let visitors pick their channel.
- Welcome without dumbing down — plain language about genuinely hard material; beginners are addressed as future contributors.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort: keep the existing prefers-reduced-motion handling and sensible contrast/keyboard behavior; no formal WCAG target.
