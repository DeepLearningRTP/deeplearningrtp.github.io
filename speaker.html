Leading a session

Everything you need to know, on one page. Short version: pick a paper, show up, talk about what you understood and what you didn't. The room does the rest.

The format

  • One paper or topic, two hours, Wednesdays at noon. Frontier RTP + Zoom, working practitioners and learners, all levels.
  • It's a guided discussion, not a lecture. Walk us through the paper for 20–30 minutes — what it claims, what surprised you, what you don't buy — and the discussion carries the rest.
  • No slides required. The paper on a screen is plenty. Honest confusion is more useful to this room than polish.
  • You don't need to be an expert on the paper. You need to have read it twice.

What we handle

  • The announcement, everywhere: the website, the calendar feed, the weekly email, Discord, and the RSVP page.
  • A ready-to-post blurb for your own LinkedIn or network — sent to you, posted by you, only if you want.
  • The room, the AV, the Zoom, the reminders.
  • Afterward: the session recap credits you permanently in the archive.

Day-of logistics

  • Arrive ~11:45. HDMI at the front of the room; the organizer runs the hybrid Zoom.
  • Lunch is bring-your-own; people eat while you talk. That's normal here.
Claim a Wednesday. Post to the group with the paper you have in mind (or ask for one off our list), or raise your hand at any session. We schedule 4–6 weeks out.
